I just wanted to provide an update on recent developments with wastewater plant
planning given that I expect an SID decision point to be coming soon.  The
decision will likely be whether the SID wants to 1) proceed with repairs of the
clarifier for approx $115k which will get us back to having a WWTF that cannot
treat ammonia and likely needs replaced in the next 6 years or 2) proceed with
a cluster septic system to be installed next Spring for $500k-$750k that will
not be regulated (no reporting to the State or EPA) and has a expected 40 year
service life.

Details:

## WWTF Clarifier needed repairs

The clarifier has been down/broken since
October 2025.  I don’t think the effluent will meet our permit requirements
while this system is down in more categories than our regular ammonia
exceedance.  

I’ve found another supplier [Supplier 1] that quoted all
needed materials (wall bearings, sprockets, shafts, chains, and skids) at
$44,475.23 excluding taxes and shipping.  I got a quote from [Supplier 2]
(a local wastewater KTR) for install of $63,342.  Adding tank cleaning and
shipping, I estimate clarifier repairs would cost $115k.  The lead time on the
chain is 16 to 18 weeks, which means repairs would happen late-2026 at the
earliest. $115k is a big improvement from the $182k supply only quote that I
had previously received. I also met another local KTR at the wastewater plant
on 9 June, who said he would check with his suppliers to see
availability/pricing/lead times and would let me know, but I’m not optimistic
that he will have much luck.

## Potential cluster septic system

On 29 May, [Professional Engineer 1] and I walked the sewer line
alignment from 40th St to the wastewater plant and determined that the
potential area for the septic system was a bit south of where I previously
envisioned, in the back/east portion of [two lots in the SID].  The
percolation tests were completed on 8 June on these properties.  Field
measurements indicated that a septic system is feasible in this location. It’s
also possible that the septic tank could be located right off 40th St, which
would allow for easy periodic pumping.
I’m waiting for [Professional Engineer 1] to complete his
system sizing and budgetary cost estimate to proceed with further discussions.
He thinks we could have design and permitting complete and be ready to
construct in Spring 2027.  The willingness of the two homeowners to sell or
provide easements will be critical if this plan were to be implemented.  

I’m anticipating a cost in the range of $500k - $750k, including
decommissioning the current facility.  I plan to reach out soon to the State to
determine if a State Revolving Fund (SRF) loan might be an option to fund this
and if so what the terms might be.

There’s more details that I can share, but I tried to provide the most
pertinent info above.  Feel free to ask questions and expect to receive another
update in the next couple weeks.

I just spoke with the Supervisor for the SRF section for the State of NE,
Steve McNulty, and he said that the soonest the State will have any available
SRF funds for wastewater projects is 2030 and that there will be a lot of
competing interests at that time.  He stated that they did not rank 2027 SRF
applications because they won’t have any available funds.  He also said that
typical SRF low-interest loan terms are 20 to 30 years, which I think would work
well for the SID.  Obviously, the lack of available SRF funds is not good news.
